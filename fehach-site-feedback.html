Section 1 — Quick read of the site

Six things I was watching for

Here's roughly what goes through a hiring manager's or consulting client's head in the first 30 seconds on a site like this — not what the site says it does, but what it communicates. The site is strong in some of these; a couple are easy opportunities.

hero
Does it stop the scroll in 5 seconds?
The hero tagline is a capability list ("Designing and delivering enterprise security, network visibility, microsegmentation…"). It reads more like a job description than a person. A small tweak to lead with an outcome could change that first impression.
proof
Does it show outcomes, not just capabilities?
Most bullets are activity-based ("Led", "Designed"). There isn't a metric on the page yet — no "reduced X from Y to Z", no "N banks secured." Adding even one or two specific results would change the texture of the whole site.
social
Is there third-party validation anywhere?
No testimonials, customer quotes, or named recognitions yet — and this is where I think there's the most opportunity. The certifications (CCIE, CISSP) are there but sit alongside "Education" rather than being framed as proof of delivery. Even one short quote from a former colleague or customer would shift how the whole site reads.
CTAs
Is there a clear path for two different visitors?
The contact section has LinkedIn + a Gmail address, but no explicit fork for "I want to hire you" vs. "I want to bring you in for a project." Easy fix — the suggestion is in Section 3F.
scan
Can a recruiter scan it in 30 seconds?
Actually pretty good here — the card grid and section structure are clean, and Cisco tenure + CCIE are easy to find. The one thing missing is a hero line that does the summarising for the reader before they have to dig in.
diff
Does it stand out from other CCIEs?
"Zero Trust, microsegmentation, AI automation" is on a lot of profiles right now. Federico's real edge — the 50+ LATAM deployments, PCI banking wins, the MBA layered on a dual CCIE, the automation tools built from scratch — isn't visible yet. That's the whole opportunity.
The site is beautifully built and technically credible — it shows Federico can ship a polished product, which is itself a signal. What it doesn't show yet is what happened when he deployed things. The gap between what he's actually done and what the site communicates is the whole opportunity here. Everything in the suggestions below is aimed at closing that gap.
